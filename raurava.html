Raurava

The Hell of Screaming

Raurava is a place in Hindu mythology where bad people go after they die. It's a place of extreme suffering for those who have done very bad things, like hurting others or breaking important rules.

Reasons for Punishment

In Hindu stories, Raurava is for people who have done terrible crimes such as murder, torture, or betrayal. These actions hurt others badly and disrupt how people should live together.

The punishment in Raurava matches how bad the crimes were. People who caused a lot of pain to others face similar pain in return, like a kind of fair punishment.

The Punishment

People in Raurava suffer a lot. They are surrounded by scary creatures that hurt them physically and mentally. The screams of pain and sadness fill the place, making it a terrifying place to be.

The suffering in Raurava isn't just punishment. It's meant to make them realize how bad their actions were and make them sorry for what they did. This is seen as a way for their soul to become better and move towards a better life in the future.

Raurava teaches the idea of karma, where every action has a consequence. It reminds people to be good and not hurt others, or else they might face similar pain later. This suffering is seen as a way to grow spiritually and become a better person in future lives.
